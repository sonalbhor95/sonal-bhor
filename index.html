Credit Card Fraud Detection using SMOTE and ADASYN

A predictive framework was developed to detect fraudulent credit card transactions in a highly imbalanced dataset, where fraud cases account for only 0.172% of all transactions. The model leverages a Random Forest Classifier combined with oversampling techniques, achieving the highest F1 score of 86.47% on the test set. Key insights include the importance of focusing on precision and recall metrics to mitigate risks associated with false negatives (undetected fraud) and false positives (genuine transactions flagged as fraudulent). This framework emphasizes the need for robust strategies to handle imbalanced datasets and ensure effective fraud detection while minimizing customer dissatisfaction.

Tools: Python, Scikit-Learn, Pandas, Matplotlib, Seaborn, Random Forest, Oversampling Techniques, SMOTE, ADASYN
